Understanding Diabetes Mellitus Type 1 and Type 2

Educate. Prevent. Manage.

🔬 What is Diabetes Mellitus?

Diabetes Mellitus (DM) is one of the most common chronic illnesses affecting millions worldwide. Whether you're a student, healthcare worker, or someone living with diabetes, this page will guide you in understanding Type 1 (DM1) and Type 2 (DM2)—their causes, symptoms, treatment, and prevention.

Diabetes Mellitus is a metabolic disorder that results in high blood sugar levels over a prolonged period due to issues with insulin production or function.

🧬 Type 1 Diabetes (DM1)

  • Autoimmune condition where the body attacks insulin-producing beta cells.
  • Usually diagnosed in children or young adults.
  • Lifelong insulin therapy required.

🍽 Type 2 Diabetes (DM2)

  • More common than Type 1.
  • Often linked to lifestyle factors such as poor diet and inactivity.
  • Can be managed or even reversed.
